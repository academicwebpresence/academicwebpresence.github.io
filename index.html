Academic Web Presence Guide & Checklist

Your web presence as a professional is becoming increasingly important. Being visible on the internet has many benefits, whether you are on the job market, looking to share your research and materials, or wanting to connect with other scholars. People and committees will Google you when you apply of jobs or give talks at a department. It is useful to spend a little time creating a professional, up-to-date, and consistent web image.

The following is a checklist of some things to consider (you can click on a heading to close the drop-down box). If you have some suggestions too, you may send an email to academicwebpresence@gmail.com.

Web Presence Checklist

Personal:

GitHub Pages is a great way to build your own permanent, professional website in HTML. It's free and shows up easily in search results, and the URL will look like https://yourname.github.io. To code the website, it may help to use free, open-source, awesome modern templates like the ones at BootStrap. Alternatively, you can create a WordPress site and pay a small amount to have your own custom URL (e.g. https://www.yourname.com).

[1] add descriptive links to other sites (department, collaborators, advisors, sources of funding, useful links, etc.).

[2] have other sites/social media accounts link to your site.

[3] keep your website up-to-date.

[4] add metadata into your HTML code. e.g. 

  <meta charset="utf-8">
  <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1, shrink-to-fit=no">
  <meta name="description" content="Your description">
  <meta name="keywords" content="Your Name, Research Area, Department, etc">
  <meta name="author" content="Your Name">

Google Analytics is useful to track how much traffic your personal webpage is getting, and from where. Create an account and add the snippet of code they provide into your HTML header to get it running.

Social Media:

Facebook. Improve your privacy settings to control what potential employers may see.

Twitter is used commonly in a number of fields to promote papers and discussion. Tweet with the goal of being constructive in mind.

Instagram is a great way to show off your artistic side and balance it with your academic one.

Accounts:

Google Scholar is a must. Make sure all your papers are found. Add your photo, subfields, and links to your homepage.

ORCID iD is a persistent digital identifier that identifies you, which an increasing number of publishers/journals use. Set up and share your iD (e.g. on your homepage).

LinkedIn is great for professional connections and looking for jobs.

GitHub is a great way to share your open-source code, and can serve as a coding portfolio.

ResearchGate is a professional network that can increase the visibility of your research and papers.

Academia also allows you to set up an account, share your work, and connect with other researchers.

Publons tracks your publications, citation metrics, peer reviews.

Mendeley is a free reference manager and an academic social network.

Academic Tree lets you see your academic family (advisors, students). For an example, see: Steven Weinberg.

Sites like WordPress.com, Github, and Stack Overflow use your Gravatar image, so set your Gravatar to your professional photo.

Other:

WikiData is used by Google to gets some of its information. It is a collaboratively edited structured knowledge database hosted by the Wikimedia Foundation. So make an account and create a personalized entry for yourself. For an example, see: Steven Weinberg.

Try google your name (also try in in-cognito mode). And Bing and Yahoo too. Make sure that you like the results. Remove obsolete sites from old institutions, personal accounts, etc. that show up.

Add links in your social/professional accounts to your personal webpage and vice versa. On your personal site, link to your department as well, and other well-known sites (e.g. sources of funding like NSF, or your advisor's site). This can help boost search results.

Use consistent images and decriptions about yourself to help promote your presence in a coherent manner.
